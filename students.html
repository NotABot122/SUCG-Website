Student Programs

Why Join SUCG

Real Experience. Real Clients. Real Growth.

SUCG is more than a club — it is a hands-on consulting experience. You will work on real consulting projects for real organizations, following a professional consulting process from kickoff through final presentation.

We select students based on professionalism, analytical curiosity, and coachability. What matters is your drive to learn and your commitment to a high standard. No formal experience is required.

What You Gain

What SUCG Offers Members

Real Client Work

Gain hands-on experience working on consulting projects with real client organizations

Targeted Training

Pre-project training covering problem definition, research methods, insight development, and presenting recommendations

Professional Standards

Work directly with clients and present analysis and recommendations at a professional standard

How to Join

From Application to Engagement

01

Information Session (1–2 weeks)

Attend a session to learn about current projects, the time commitment, and what the experience involves.

02

Submit Your Application (1 week)

Applications focus on your background, motivations, and relevant experience.

03

Interview (1 week)

A short professional interview focused on communication, analytical thinking, and coachability. Decisions released by end of week.

04

Training (2 weeks)

Selected consultants complete targeted training before client engagement begins.

05

Project Work (6 weeks)

Work on a real client project with faculty and mentor support, delivering a polished final presentation at close.

Candidate Profile

The Ideal SUCG Candidate

We are looking for students who are reliable, communicate clearly, think critically, and are willing to learn. Consulting work requires people who take commitments seriously, ask thoughtful questions, and follow through on their work.

SUCG is open to students from all majors. The strongest teams bring together different perspectives, including business, liberal arts, science, and technology. That mix helps us approach problems from multiple angles and develop stronger solutions.
