Client Services

What We Do

Strategic Thinking, No Strings Attached

SUCG partners with organizations of all sizes to deliver structured research, analysis, and insights.

Our student consultants bring fresh perspectives, analytical skills, and actionable solutions to each project. Whether you are looking for strategic direction or tactical support, SUCG delivers at no cost to your organization.

All we need is three one-hour meetings over an 11-week quarter, and you will get a polished report you can act on immediately.

What to Expect

Our Process

01

Initial Conversation (Week 1)

A collaborative discussion to better understand your organization, its challenges, and what success looks like for you.

02

Team Assembly & Training (Weeks 2–5)

We recruit and train a student team specifically for your project, ensuring the right skills and mindset before work begins.

03

Kickoff Meeting (Week 6)

A one-hour session to align on goals, constraints, available data, and key questions.

04

Research & Analysis (2 weeks)

The student team conducts structured research and develops analytical insights, all under faculty and mentor oversight.

05

Midpoint Check-In (Week 8)

We provide preliminary findings for your review and incorporate your feedback to guide the final stretch of our analysis.

06

Final Presentation (Week 11)

A polished presentation of research, analysis, and strategic recommendations — yours to keep and act on.

Why Partner With Us

Real Value, Minimal Ask

Your time is valuable. We designed our model with that in mind — three hours over an 11-week period in exchange for actionable strategic insights.

Students are selected for professionalism and analytical curiosity, then trained specifically for your project. They operate with the same accountability and professionalism you would expect from a junior consultant, all backed by Seattle University faculty oversight.

Beyond the report itself, partnering with SUCG connects you to Seattle University's talent pipeline. This is an opportunity to identify strong candidates before they graduate.
